Understand who I am as a person

Hi my name is Patrick Hunt. I trained as an Architectural Technician and Architect and hold Degrees in both. I qualified in 2009 when there was little Architectural work and had time to think about other areas that interest me. I started vegetable gardening and spending lots of time outdoors in nature and became fascinated with the natural world. I was fortunate to discover Permaculture Design which has influenced my gardening and career path ever since. I became interested in the idea of teaching after a few years gardening at home. At the time I wrote a blog of projects I enjoyed doing and sharing.

My first teaching role was assisting on a fetac level 4 Horticultural Course through the National Learning Network in Stillorgan. This I really enjoyed working with adults in a garden setting. In 2014 my wife Laura and I went traveling around Europe by campervan and on our return in 2015 I became self employed teaching Gardening and nature to children in primary schools.Fortunately, I was accepted onto the panel with the heritage in schools scheme and today much of my work is part funded through the Heritage Council.
I have been going into schools and bringing children and teachers out into nature for 4 years now and I love my job. The children are innately interested and it is important that they make a strong connection with nature early on. As they go through life they will retain this connection to nature and care for and protect it. We moved out of Dublin and have settled in West Wicklow near Dunlavin where we grow as much of our own food as we can, gardening with wildlife in mind. Services

School Visits: Biodiversity and Habitats

Explore the Biodiversity and Habitats around your school and local area with a themed visit. Some of the themes for my visits are Native trees and shrubs, Wildflowers, Bees, Minibeasts, Pond Dipping ,Birdwatching and Irish Mammals.

School Gardening

Seasonal Gardening visits where the pupils learn about growing food, flowers, trees and shrubs. These are practical gardening session allowing the children and teachers to gain some experience with sowing, potting on and caring for plants.

Garden Design

The school may need some guidance with the layout and plant choice to include in the garden. Arrange a consultation visit where we can asses the grounds and make suggestions for elements such as hedgerow, insect hotels, outdoor classroom, wildflower areas, composting , fruit trees, vegetable bed layouts and sourcing materials.

Class field trips

Visit a local woodland, grassland, pond, river, bog, fen or seashore to explore Irish Flora and Fauna. This can form part of an educational school tour.

Community

Guided nature walks: Tidy towns and local groups requiring a guided nature walk to explore what is in their local area. Themed talks on Birds, Trees, Wildflowers, and Wildlife. Duration approx. 1.5 hours at a suitable location.

Monthly Gardening Class

Join my monthly gardening class entitled Growing Food in the Home Garden. Gardening is a yearly cycle and the classes is designed to assist the learner in what to do and when to do it through the seasons. The class has a strong emphasis on food growing but does also address pollinator friendly plants and habitat creation with a holistic approach to year round interest in the garden.
